Political Positions

Because strawmans are too hard to fucking handle.

Why I made this blog/post

Because politics really suck when other people don't really even argue with your actual positions.

Whenever actual political positions are argued, there is always a high risk of getting your own positions completely misunderstood, something that has happened to me over the past few months whenever I've tried to debate policies and political positions.

In this blog I want to clear out any kind of misunderstanding that people might have about my actual political positions, because the last thing I want is to have people arguing about a strawman of me, and not my actual ideas.

I also want to make this blog with the objective of keeping up with my own political positions, in order to try to be as consistent and charitable as possible when it comes to debates.

Current online landscape when it comes to political debates

The sad truth is that there have been many instances in which the online political landscape is disappointing, but there have also been many instances in which fruitful conversations can arise.

I think everyone's main goal when it comes to political ideas should be to try to push for reasonable policies grounded in facts that can allow us to improve as a society

I believe that most people don't really think that much when it comes to the policies that they are trying to push for, aswell as a lack of philosophical grounding that would allow them to defend those positions according to moral axioms.

I think it is of high importance to try to understand the basics of a few other areas before actually engaging with political ideas. A few resources I would highly encourage to check out are:

  • Crash Course - Philosophy
  • Crash Course - Economics
  • Crash Course - Sociology
  • Unlearning Economics
  • ContraPoints
  • Eco Gecko
  • Stanford Encyclopedia Of Philosophy

When it comes to reading theory about communism/anarchism, I think it is very important for people to read theory,but at the same time not reading theory shouldn't necessarily be a gate-keeping instrument, as nowadays information can be found in many different flavours than only reading theory.

Moral Axioms

I believe that it is important for everyone to first understand their respective moral axioms through philosophical means, because doing so will enable you to engage with politics in a much profound way.

I don't have a college-level education when it comes to philosophy, however, thanks to the use of different resources and informing myself about different moral frameworks I've been able to produce what I think is a consistent moral framework

My two main moral axioms are:

  • Freedom
  • Equality

Philosophical Foundations

  • Part 1
    1. I exist.
    2. I have an experience.
    3. I want to maximize my experience.
  • Part 2
    1. Others exist.
    2. Others have an experience.
    3. Others want to maximize their experience.
  • Part 3
    1. Humans helping and co-existing with one another create better experiences.
    2. If I help and co-exist with others, it will maximize my experience.
    3. Others will help and co-exist with me to maximize their experience.

Abortion

More info about abortion

I believe that abortion shouldn't be regulated, and I'm a pro-choice individual

While I think we all consider that life begins at conception, I think the main question that needs to be asked is about whether the fetus has 'personhood' or not.

In my opinion, applying personhood to a fetus is something that would make things very difficult aswell as complicate several processes such as:

  • If the fetus truly has personhood, then, in case the person carrying the fetus commits a crime, would that mean that the person can't be arrested because the fetus would be illegally arrested?
  • If the fetus truly has personhood, would that mean that all human rights apply to them?

Aswell, I think we have to take into account that bodily autonomy is also a valid argument in favour of abortion: even if someone provokes a car crash and injures someone in the process, and then the perpetrator is connected to the victim's body in order to keep them alive, the perpetrator can still make the decision about whether he wants to keep connected to the other person or not, because bodily autonomy is already highly prioritized in most countries.

We also have to take into account that the quality of life is probably way more important than the 'sanctity' of life
